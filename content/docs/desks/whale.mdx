**ERC-8004 tokenId 11.** The whale desk watches what the **best traders alive** are actually doing — not
a static watchlist, but a **dynamic, per-asset** discovery off the Hyperliquid leaderboard (a public pool
of ~38k traders). It's cloud-native: Hyperliquid is reachable from Railway, so this runs live in
production.

## What it reads

`firm/hl_whales.py`:

- **Leaderboard discovery** — pulls the top traders by realized PnL from Hyperliquid's public leaderboard.
- **Live positions** — for the **specific asset being traded**, reads those top traders' current position
  side, size, and ROE.
- **Aggregate stance** — turns the cohort's positioning into a long / short / mixed whale stance.

## Why dynamic beats a watchlist

A hard-coded whale list goes stale — wallets blow up, rotate, or get dethroned. By re-discovering the
**current** top-N every cycle, the desk always tracks who is *winning now*, and it reads positions in the
exact asset under consideration rather than a generic "smart money" blob.

## Honest caveats

- **MNT isn't on Hyperliquid**, so for Mantle-native assets this desk yields nothing — the
  [On-chain/Risk desk](/docs/desks/onchain-risk) covers Mantle DEX flow instead. The whale desk is
  strongest on majors and HL-listed assets (BTC, HYPE, SOL).
- Following whales is a **positioning context**, not a guarantee — even top traders are wrong, and there's
  latency between their fill and our read. It folds into `desk_performance` and earns its weight like any
  other desk.
