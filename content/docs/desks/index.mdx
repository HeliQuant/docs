## How the desks divide the problem

Nine desks, three jobs: **predict** a pattern, **follow** informed actors, or supply **context**. The
firm tilts which family leads based on each asset's measured character (see below).

## How they collaborate

The PM does **not** average the desks. Each desk earns a **reliability weight** from its own track
record; the PM ranks them, tags TRUSTED / DISCOUNT, runs a bull-vs-bear debate over the outputs, and only
then decides — behind a hard R:R gate.

## Which desks lead depends on the asset

`asset_efficiency.py` profiles each asset **fresh** every cycle (volatility + return-autocorrelation). A
**predictable** asset (autocorrelation ≥ 0.10, or a validated edge) boosts the **prediction** desks
(Regime, OI-Contrarian); an **efficient** asset (autocorrelation < 0.05, no exploitable pattern) boosts
the **flow** desks instead. Honest current read: **all majors are efficient right now** — even MNT's old
pattern has decayed — so flow desks lead and prediction desks are discounted.

## The ML is the Regime desk

We tested a directional ML (XGBoost BUY/SELL on MNT) — it scored **~51% accuracy and −50% to −62% OOS
net of fees** (Finding #22), so it is **not** used. The ML that *does* run is the **regime classifier**
(82.6% OOS) inside the [Regime/Technical desk](/docs/desks/regime), used as a **capital allocator / risk
read**, not a price predictor. Honest ML: the good model earns its place; the disproven one stays out.

## Supporting inputs (not weighted desks)

  (the quadrant above).
- **Carry** — delta-neutral funding-carry monitor.

## Reliability weights

The PM weights desks by **learned reliability** (`desk_performance`): each desk earns a weight
`0.6–1.4` from its realized track record (stance vs realized direction, ≥15 samples). Neutral until
proven, never muted. See [Self-learning](/docs/self-learning).
