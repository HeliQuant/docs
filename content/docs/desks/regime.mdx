**ERC-8004 tokenId 3.** This is **the ML desk** — and it's where HeliQuant's honesty about machine
learning lives. It runs the model that *earned* its place (a regime classifier) and explicitly does
**not** run the one that didn't (a directional predictor).

## What it reads

`firm/organization.py · tool_regime()` over fresh 1h candles:

- **XGBoost regime classifier** — `82.6%` out-of-sample accuracy (`91.6%` on high-confidence calls).
- **ADX trend strength** vs a dynamic trending threshold.
- **Dynamic risk/reward** recommendation derived from the regime.
- **OOS-validation status** for the asset (`is_validated` against the validated-edge registry).

## The honest ML story

We tested a **directional** ML (XGBoost predicting BUY/SELL on MNT). It scored **~51% accuracy and
−50% to −62% OOS net of fees** (Finding #22) — no better than a coin flip after costs. It is **not used
and not registered on-chain.** A full-history (2.9 yr) walk-forward + rolling re-tune of trend-following
was also flat-to-negative OOS; the earlier "+5.58%" was a short-window artifact, since scrubbed.

What *is* accurate is the **regime classifier** (82.6% OOS). But an accurate regime label is **not** a
trading profit — so it's used as a **capital allocator / risk read** feeding the PM, never as a price
oracle. The good model earns its place; the disproven one stays out. That distinction is the differentiator.
