**ERC-8004 tokenId 10.** flow-intel is a **self-learning desk**: it doesn't assume any flow signal works
— it continuously *tests* a basket of them (OI, funding, order-flow, momentum) and only lets the survivors
earn weight. It's the desk that embodies the firm's "an edge must be earned" rule.

## What it reads

`firm/flow_intel.py` — a rotating set of flow-signal hypotheses, each forward-logged and scored:

- **OI** — open-interest change behaviour.
- **Funding** — perp funding-rate extremes.
- **Order-flow** — aggressor imbalance.
- **Momentum** — short-horizon continuation.

Each candidate is held to a **cost-aware, out-of-sample** standard and a **Benjamini-Hochberg FDR** gate
so that running many tests doesn't manufacture a false winner. Today **0–1 of 4** validate — and the desk
reports that honestly rather than forcing a signal.

## Why FDR matters

If you test 20 signals at p < 0.05, you expect ~1 to look "significant" by pure chance. A naive
best-of-N search **guarantees** a false edge. The Benjamini-Hochberg procedure controls the
false-discovery rate across the whole battery, so a signal that clears the gate is far likelier to be
real. This same FDR discipline is shared with `edge_lab` and the onboarding screen.

## Honest caveats

flow-intel is **mostly a producer of "no"** — and that's the point. It exists to stop the firm from
trading flow folklore. When it does validate a signal, that signal folds into `desk_performance` and is
ranked alongside the other desks; until then it abstains. See [Self-learning](/docs/self-learning).
