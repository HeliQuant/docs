**ERC-8004 tokenId 9.** This is the **disciplined exception to "abstain."** Most desks supply context;
this one carries the firm's single **OOS-validated trading edge** — fading crowded perp open-interest —
but it only fires when *both* a registry-validated edge **and** a live signal align.

## What it reads

`firm/organization.py · tool_oi_contrarian()`:

- **Validated-edge registry** — `data/validated_edges.json`, produced by a **cost-aware OOS backtest**
  (`scripts/39`). Holds `p_win`, `payoff_b`, `sample_n`, `oos_roi_pct` per asset that survived.
- **Live OI-contrarian signal** — perp **OI 24h-change quintiles**: top quintile → **SHORT**, bottom
  quintile → **LONG** (fade the crowd).

## Honest caveats

This is a **real but fragile** edge: small sample (n≈34), hedge-like (it shines when the crowd is wrong,
often in down moves), and **inconsistent fold-to-fold**. So it's fractional-Kelly sized with hard
risk caps — never bet as a sure thing. Crucially, **the registry is allowed to be empty**: when an asset's
edge decays out of validation (as MNT's has on fresh full-history data), this desk degrades to
**context only** and the firm goes back to abstaining. The edge must stay *earned*, every re-test.
