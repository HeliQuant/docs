HeliQuant learns at **three layers**. Losses aren't wasted — they're the training signal. The system is
allowed to hold an **empty** validated-edge registry; promoting a dead edge would be the failure.

## The loop

Every closed decision feeds back into the three layers, and each layer changes how the *next* decision is
made — what edge is trusted, which desks are weighted, and how each trade is sized, throttled, and timed.

## Layer 1 — Edge discovery ("earn the edge")

The `edge_lab` tests a **hypothesis library** (OI-change, price-momentum, funding, order-flow imbalance ×
contrarian/momentum) against an asset's real perp history under **one gate**:

- cost-aware **out-of-sample** (round-trip ~20 bps: fee + spread + slippage),
- **5-fold walk-forward**,
- **outlier-robustness** (drop the single best fold — the edge must still hold),
- **Benjamini–Hochberg FDR** correction (kills best-of-N false positives).

An edge graduates to `validated_edges.json` (gates real aggression) only when *earned across folds*;
otherwise it sits in `candidate_edges.json` on **probation**, requiring confirmation on consecutive
**new-data** cycles. Today the validated registry is empty — MNT's flagship edge **decayed and was
demoted** (Finding #11). Candidates (e.g. HYPE oi-contrarian, SUI funding-fade) await fresh-data proof.

## Layer 2 — Desk reliability

`desk_performance` scores each desk's stance vs realized direction and assigns a reliability weight
`0.6–1.4` (≥15 samples, else neutral). The PM weights proven desks heavily and discounts unreliable ones
— fighting "too many desks = noise". Weights are an **advisory prior**; the gates still bind.

## Layer 3 — Tactical re-map (the live floor)

The continuous campaign learns run-to-run — and it acts on what it learns by changing **size, frequency,
and hold-time**, concentrating capital on what works and starving what bleeds:

- **cond_record** — every closed trade dials a condition's throttle; a condition with a proven-losing
  record **in that regime** trades at half size (regime-aware: a short that bled in an up-market isn't
  penalised in a down-market).
- **failed_conditions** — a losing round benches a condition with a TTL (forgiven later, so it can
  re-learn).
- **asset-performance ladder** — capital *and* frequency tilt to what works: a **cold** asset (losing
  record) trades **half size** *and* opens only **1 of 3 steps**; a **deep-cold** one is **benched**
  (periodic probe); a **warm** asset is **boosted** — `1.3×` for a decent proven record (≥55% win, ≥8
  trades), `1.6×` for a **strong** one (≥66% win) — always **capped below** the `2×` reserved for a
  validated edge. (A hot recent record earns more capital; it is not mistaken for an edge.)
- **calibrated TP/SL** — TP/SL distances are *measured* per asset from realized excursions (Maximum
  Favourable / Adverse Excursion), not guessed. The old fixed 2.5×-ATR TP was reached by only **0–21%**
  of trades — so a reachable, per-asset TP replaced it (`scripts/95`).
- **trailing stop** — a trend-follow trade ratchets its stop toward price so a genuine run isn't capped at
  a fixed TP; contrarian/fade trades keep a fixed TP (meant to snap, not run).
- **dynamic horizon** — the hold-time adapts to the setup type: a **trend-follow** trade is held longer
  (8h, trends persist), a **contrarian/fade** is short (3h, it snaps fast or the thesis is wrong), a
  **validated edge** runs (24h).
- **clear-trend gate** — the floor only trades a **clear** trend, aligned to it; a flat/ambiguous regime
  **abstains**, and a counter-trend signal flips to the trend-following side.

## Learning from abstention (it learns even when it *doesn't* trade)

Most systems learn only from trades. HeliQuant also scores its **abstentions**: when the clear-trend gate
declines a setup, it logs a **shadow** of the would-be trade and judges it at its horizon against real
price — was abstaining **right** (the skipped trade would have lost) or **wrong** (it would have won)?
Aggregated per gate (`shadow_record`, surfaced on `/campaign` as `shadow_learning`), this turns idle and
cooldown time into signal: the firm measures whether its own discipline actually pays, and can tell an
over-restrictive gate from a protective one. It's scored against **real candles**, so it isn't self-flattery.

## The honest spine

> Promote on evidence, confirm before you trust, demote when it decays. That's the line between
> self-learning and self-deception. New hypotheses — including ML — must out-earn the gate; none gets a
> free pass.
