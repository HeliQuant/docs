> **HeliQuant is a trading *firm*, not a model.** Nine specialist desks ingest real data and debate;
> a Portfolio Manager sparks **ENTER only on a validated edge** — otherwise it holds gear (**ABSTAIN**).
> Every decision is sealed on-chain. We publish what *doesn't* work too.

## The one-line pitch

Most "AI traders" show you a +340% backtest and a black box. HeliQuant shows you the **discipline**: a
firm of AI agents that earns every edge under cost-aware out-of-sample testing, retires edges when they
decay, anchors its decisions on Mantle, and **abstains** when there's nothing real to trade.

## What makes it different

- **A firm, not a single model.** Regime/ML, Macro (Allora), On-chain/Risk, Research, Smart-Money,
  Smart-Social, OI-Contrarian + Flow-Intel + Whale desks debate; the PM synthesizes.
- **Honest by design.** Deterministic code computes the numbers; LLMs debate; nothing ships unless it
  survives **cost-aware out-of-sample** + walk-forward + an outlier-robustness check.
- **Verifiable on Mantle.** 10 ERC-8004 agent identities, every desk output sealed as a credential,
  every trade's outcome anchored in a transaction — auditable on Mantlescan.
- **It abstains.** When no validated edge applies, holding is the feature — not a bug.

## The honesty rule

> Every number on this site traces to a real run, probe, or on-chain transaction. When an edge decays,
> we **demote it** — the validated-edge registry is allowed to be empty. The discipline that retires a
> dead flagship *is* the product.
