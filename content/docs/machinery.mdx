The [nine desks](/docs/desks) only *produce reads*. Turning those reads into a disciplined, recorded,
self-improving decision is the job of a whole **machinery of non-desk roles** — the parts of HeliQuant
that act like the rest of a real trading firm: a steward who keeps the data healthy, researchers who
argue, a PM who decides, a floor that trades, a risk officer who sizes, and a back office that records
and learns.

## The merge — the floor proposes, the LLM PM disposes

HeliQuant has two engines: the slow **LLM org** (the full desks then debate then PM cycle, analyse-only)
and the fast **no-LLM floor** (`campaign.py`, a continuous paper trader). They used to be *independent* —
the floor traded purely on its own quantitative gates while the LLM only analysed.

As of the **PM sign-off merge** (`firm/pm_signoff.py` + the gate in `campaign.py`, search
`PM SIGN-OFF (THE MERGE)`, line 917) they are **coupled at the decision**: the floor still *proposes* a
trade from its quantitative gates, but the **LLM Portfolio Manager now signs off — APPROVE or VETO — on
every candidate** as the **final gate** before a position opens. It is fed the **live floor signals**
(trend, stretch, TV 1h-vs-1D, range-position, whale stance, size, SL/TP) — *fresh*, not the org's stale
feature CSVs — and it is **one** LLM call (provider Groq, rotating model pool), not the full org cycle.

Crucially it **fails open** (approve) on any 429 / outage (`pm_signoff.py:47`) — the algorithmic gates
already vetted the setup, so an LLM hiccup never freezes the floor. The verdict is recorded on the
position (`p["pm"]`, `campaign.py:942`) and surfaced on `/campaign`. Toggle with `PM_SIGNOFF`
(default on, `pm_signoff.py:28`).

> **The floor proposes; the LLM disposes.** This is what makes the LLM genuinely *drive* trades — not
> just narrate them. It does **not** manufacture a profit edge: the directional book is still
> ~breakeven (no validated edge). The one validated positive edge remains delta-neutral funding-carry
> (HYPE **+5.8%/yr** walk-forward, `scripts/79`).

### Steward — keeps the watchlist healthy
`organization.py · run_operations / STEWARD_SYS` (line 353, 418). The steward reads a deterministic
**whale-watchlist health report** (how many wallets still qualify, data age, losers) and decides one
thing: **MIGRATE** (re-scan fresh Mantle DEX activity via GeckoTerminal and replace under-performers) or
**KEEP**. It does **not** pick which assets to analyze — the cycle's asset is chosen in `app.py` by simple
rotation (`ASSETS[cycles % len(ASSETS)]`, `app.py:132`). The steward's job is purely data hygiene: never
mirror stale or loss-making wallets.

- **In:** `wm.evaluate(wm.load())` health report (deterministic).
- **Process:** one LLM call judges the numbers (`STEWARD_SYS`); on `migrate`, `wm.migrate()` re-scans
  GeckoTerminal. Fails to a deterministic rule if the LLM is unavailable.
- **Out:** `{action, confidence, reason}` + before/after health.

### Researchers — the bull and the bear
`organization.py · run_debate` (line 494). Two researchers argue over the desk outputs in **two rounds**
(opening + rebuttal) on a deep model (Groq `gpt-oss-120b`, Kimi K2.6 as Plan B). This surfaces dissent
*before* the PM sees it, so a one-sided desk consensus gets stress-tested instead of rubber-stamped.

- **In:** desk tool outputs + the LLM desk stances.
- **Process:** `BULL_SYS` / `BEAR_SYS` open, then `*_REBUT_SYS` rebut (4 LLM calls total).
- **Out:** `{bull, bear, bull_rebuttal, bear_rebuttal}`.

### Portfolio Manager — the decision
`organization.py · run_pm / PM_SYS` (line 373, 547). The PM weighs the desks **ranked by learned
reliability** (`_ranked_desk_view`), reads the debate, and outputs `ENTER` (only on the one validated-edge
exception) or `ABSTAIN`. `finalize_decision` (line 672) then attaches a **gated trade ticket**; if the
ticket can't clear the R:R gate it **re-prompts the PM up to twice**, then overrides to a disciplined
**ABSTAIN** — never up to a trade.

- **In:** ranked desk stances + debate + regime status + advisory briefs (memory, carry, flow, whale,
  Mantle fundamentals).
- **Process:** one deep LLM call; then `build_trade_ticket` gates the result (repair loop, max 2).
- **Out:** `{decision, direction, confidence, reasoning, desk_consensus, trade_ticket, ticket_note}`.

## The trading floor

- **Campaign** (`campaign.py · step`, line 570) — a continuous **paper floor** at live prices. It computes
  its **own deterministic desk votes** (`desk_votes`, line 310 — flow-intel + z-score positioning extremes
  + the Hyperliquid whale read; **no LLM**), then every candidate must clear an ordered
  **[entry-quality pipeline](/docs/architecture#the-entry-quality-pipeline)** — macro de-risk, deep-cold
  bench, cold-cadence, mid-range chop, clear-trend, don't-fight-trend flip, **TV 1h-vs-1D**, **smart-money
  whale veto**, **over-extension**, and finally the **LLM PM sign-off** (the merge, above). Approved trades
  are sized on a **warm/cold ladder** with per-asset calibrated stops. It runs even when the org abstains,
  so there's always a live, honest track record (metrics at `/performance`).
- **Exploration** (`exploration.py`) — when the org **ABSTAINs**, a small paper-hunt **budget** opens
  exploratory positions on flow consensus, resolves them at 24h, and feeds the outcome to the learning
  loop. Abstaining doesn't mean idle — it means *hunting carefully*.

## Risk sizing — the trade ticket
`trade_ticket.py · size_position` (line 125). Every ENTER is sized by `size_position`, whose
`notional = risk$ × (entry / stop-distance)` mirrors the backtested engine. It picks **SAFE** (the bounded
conviction band, 0.5–1.5% risk) or **AGGRESSIVE** (fractional Kelly — the `kelly_fraction_star` *output
field*) and clamps with hard caps: **≤ 3% risk, ≤ 5× leverage**, plus a **20% drawdown breaker** that
forces SAFE. AGGRESSIVE is unlocked *only* when an OOS-validated edge's live signal fires with ≥ 20
samples; crowding only ever sizes **down**.

## The learning loop
`desk_performance` (reliability weights, `0.6–1.4`, ≥ 15 samples) · `edge_lab` (validates candidate edges
OOS with walk-forward + outlier-robustness + Benjamini–Hochberg FDR) · `cond_record` (regime-aware
tactical memory on the floor) · `edge_evolver` (offline search for variants when an edge decays). Together
they make the firm **self-improving** — desks that call it right gain weight, edges that decay lose
validation. This is its own subsystem; see [Self-learning](/docs/self-learning) for the full treatment.

## The back office — recording & persistence
- **On-chain recorders** (`onchain_recorder`, `agent_onchain`) — seal each desk's output and the decision
  hash on Mantle every cycle (see [On-chain](/docs/on-chain)).
- **State & memory** (`state_store`, `memory_store`) — persist the firm's state, decisions, campaign book,
  and ledger to **Supabase** (`hq_state` / `decisions_hq`) so nothing is lost across Railway redeploys.
- **Strategy lifecycle** (`strategy_lifecycle`) — *offline research* tooling that promotes/demotes whole
  strategies by status as their evidence strengthens or decays. It is imported by offline scripts, **not**
  by the live `organization.py` loop — so it documents the lifecycle, it doesn't run inside a cycle.
