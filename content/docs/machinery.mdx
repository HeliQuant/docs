The [nine desks](/docs/desks) only *produce reads*. Turning those reads into a disciplined, recorded,
self-improving decision is the job of a whole **machinery of non-desk roles** — the parts of HeliQuant
that act like the rest of a real trading firm: a steward who sets the agenda, researchers who argue, a PM
who decides, a floor that trades, a risk officer who sizes, and a back office that records and learns.

### Steward — sets the agenda
`organization.py · STEWARD_SYS`. Before any desk runs, the steward reads the **watchlist health report**
(which assets have fresh data, which whales still qualify) and decides **what's worth analyzing this
cycle**. It keeps the firm from burning a cycle on stale or junk assets.

### Researchers — the bull and the bear
`organization.py · run_debate`. Two researchers argue over the desk outputs in **two rounds** (opening +
rebuttal) on a deep model. This surfaces dissent *before* the PM sees it, so a one-sided desk consensus
gets stress-tested instead of rubber-stamped.

### Portfolio Manager — the decision
`organization.py · run_pm / PM_SYS`. The PM weighs the desks by **learned reliability**, reads the debate,
and applies the hard **R:R ≥ 2:1** gate to output `ENTER` (only on a validated edge) or `ABSTAIN`.
`finalize_decision` then validates the output — anything malformed or unjustified is **repaired down to
ABSTAIN**, never up to a trade.

## The trading floor

- **Campaign** (`campaign.py · step`) — a continuous **paper floor** at live prices, driven by the same
  desk votes. Every setup must clear an ordered **[entry-quality pipeline](/docs/architecture#the-entry-quality-pipeline)**
  — macro de-risk, mid-range chop, clear-trend, **TV 1h-vs-1D alignment**, **smart-money whale veto**, and
  **over-extension** — then it's sized on a warm/cold ladder with per-asset calibrated stops. It runs even
  when the org abstains, so there's always a live, honest track record (metrics at `/performance`).
- **Exploration** (`exploration.py`) — when the org **ABSTAINs**, a small paper-hunt **budget** opens
  exploratory positions on flow consensus, resolves them at 24h, and feeds the outcome to the learning
  loop. Abstaining doesn't mean idle — it means *hunting carefully*.

## Risk sizing — the trade ticket
`trade_ticket.py · size_position`. Every ENTER is sized by **fractional Kelly** (`kelly_fraction_star`),
then clamped by hard caps — **≤ 3% risk, ≤ 5× leverage**, with a 20% drawdown breaker — and split into
**SAFE vs AGGRESSIVE** profiles. AGGRESSIVE is unlocked *only* when an OOS-validated edge's live signal
fires.

## The learning loop
`desk_performance` (reliability weights) · `edge_lab` (validates candidate edges OOS) · `cond_record`
(regime-aware tactical memory) · `edge_evolver` (searches variants when an edge is demoted). Together they
make the firm **self-improving** — desks that call it right gain weight, edges that decay lose validation.
This is its own subsystem; see [Self-learning](/docs/self-learning) for the full treatment.

## The back office — recording & persistence
- **On-chain recorders** (`onchain_recorder`, `agent_onchain`) — seal each desk's output and the decision
  hash on Mantle every cycle (see [On-chain](/docs/on-chain)).
- **State & memory** (`state_store`, `memory_store`) — persist the firm's state, decisions, and ledger to
  **Supabase** so nothing is lost across redeploys.
- **Strategy lifecycle** (`strategy_lifecycle`) — promotes/demotes whole strategies by status as their
  evidence strengthens or decays.
