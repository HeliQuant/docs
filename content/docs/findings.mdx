## On Smart Money & Whales — the Mantle reality

**1. Mantle's smart money is genuinely sparse — confirmed across 4 premium sources.**
Etherscan, Cielo, CoinAlyze *and* Nansen all converge: only **~1–2 individual smart-money traders** are
active on Mantle's core assets. It's not a data-access gap — the activity isn't there yet. So HeliQuant
doesn't fake "whale-following" on Mantle; it tracks what's real.

**2. The dynamic Whale-vs-Contract insight.**
*Who holds a token* decides the signal. EOA-rich assets (BTC/ETH) → track individual whales. Contract-
heavy assets (Mantle LSTs) → track *contract flows*. HeliQuant measures holder composition on-chain
(`eth_getCode`) and **auto-selects the mode per asset**.

**3. You can't track whales one wallet at a time anymore.**
~80% of transactions route through private mempools; ghost-deposits and wallet-splitting are standard.
HeliQuant uses **aggregate cohort flow** (manipulation-robust), not single-address alerts.

**4. mETH staking is the real Mantle conviction signal.**
While individual-whale data is thin, **~$358M is locked in mETH staking** — a hard-to-fake on-chain
conviction metric worth tracking.

## On Alpha & Strategies — what's real, what isn't

**5. Accuracy ≠ Profit.** Our ML regime classifier reads regime at **82–88% out-of-sample accuracy** —
yet trading those predictions did *not* profit in simulation. Classification accuracy is not cost-
adjusted profit. So the regime engine's real job is **capital allocation & risk**, not price prediction.

**6. No magic price-TA alpha — and we say so.** Trend-following and mean-reversion both **fail rigorous
walk-forward OOS**. Eye-catching single-window returns died under full-history testing.

**7. The one real edge (then): OI-Contrarian, hedge-like.** Open-interest build-up mean-reverts.
Contrarian positioning produced **+47–64% aggregate OOS while the market fell ~60%** — an anti-correlated
hedge. Caveat: amplified by a bear environment and fold-inconsistent — deployed as a risk-controlled
hedge, not a guarantee.

**8. Funding-capture doesn't work here.** Funding rates are too small and flip sign too often — fees eat
the carry (a naive version lost 30–39%). Tested, measured, rejected.

**11. We validated an edge on MNT — then our own self-learning RETIRED it.** The OI-contrarian signal
once cleared the cost-aware OOS bar on a one-year MNT window (+25.1% net). On **fresh full-history data**
the edge had **decayed** to −7.2% OOS. The loop **demoted it on the evidence** — the validated registry
is now **empty**. The edge was never the product — *the discipline that retires a decayed flagship is.*

## On Execution & Risk

**12. A trade is a hypothesis with an invalidation — gated, not guessed.** Every decision becomes a
ticket: entry zone, a **structural stop**, an invalidation, a TP ladder, and a hard **R:R ≥ 2:1 gate**.
The LLM judges direction; deterministic code computes the numbers; the gate enforces safety.

**13. Aggression earned by data.** SAFE by default; sizes up to AGGRESSIVE (fractional-Kelly, capped 3%
risk / 5× leverage, 20% drawdown breaker) **only** when an OOS-validated edge's live signal fires.

**14. Verifiable on Mantle — the decision ledger, on-chain.** Each decision's hash is anchored in a live
Mantle transaction under a **broadcast-on-ENTER** policy. On-chain is the tamper-proof audit trail.

## On Method — the differentiator

**9. Honesty-by-design.** Deterministic tools compute; LLMs debate; the system **rejects any strategy
that doesn't survive OOS**. We even retracted our own earlier headline numbers when they failed.

**15. We rejected our own +96% backtest.** A market-neutral mETH/ETH convergence strategy showed +96%
OOS / 95.6% win — but under realistic slippage it turns to **−73 bps per trade**, a thin-liquidity
artifact. We threw it out.

**16. Edge fired — and the firm still held.** In a live MNT run the OI-Contrarian edge fired an
actionable LONG. The PM still **ABSTAINED**: the R:R gate couldn't clear and macro read Extreme Fear.

**17. We hunted four more assets — and earned none.** Cost-aware OOS on BTC/ETH/mETH/HYPE: BTC's OI edge
is **eaten by fees**, ETH/SOL flip to momentum and lose, mETH has no liquid perp, HYPE's OI moves *with*
price (momentum, not contrarian). Only MNT cleared the bar — and it has since decayed (see #11).

**18. AGGRESSIVE proven as a live truth-table.** High-conviction unlocks if-and-only-if a validated edge
(≥20 trades) *and* the account isn't past a 20% drawdown — proven as a 4-case truth-table on real data.

**19. 39% win rate, still +24% out-of-sample.** Funded $1,000, trading the validated edge OOS with live
AGGRESSIVE sizing, HeliQuant won only 13/33 (39.4%) — and finished at **$1,243.81 (+24.4%)**, net of
fees on real Bybit data. Profit from **payoff asymmetry**, not hit-rate.

**20. An asset-onboarding lab that held back a +92% candidate.** Onboarding tests a hypothesis library
under one gate: cost-aware OOS + 5-fold walk-forward + outlier-robustness (drop the best fold). HYPE's
order-flow-contrarian flashed +92% OOS — but ~65% came from one fold; drop it → +1.9%. So we **held it
back**. The registry grows only when an edge is *earned across folds*.

**21. Validation isn't claimed — it's anchored on Mantle.** When an edge clears the gate, its validation
record (asset, edge, p_win, payoff, sample_n, OOS-ROI, tokenId) is hashed and anchored in a Mantle tx.
"Validated" is **auditable, not asserted**.

**22. We tested ML too — no free pass.** RandomForest + XGBoost to predict 24h direction, traded OOS
under the same gate: **~51% accuracy, −50% to −62% OOS net of fees** → abstain. ML plugs in as just
another signal, judged by the same evidence. *(The good ML — the regime classifier — stays as a capital
allocator, not a price predictor.)*

**23. Self-learning, proven on LIVE-fetched data.** We re-ran the gate on fresh HYPE data; the edge
**crossed (+142% OOS, walk-forward robust)** — the loop responded to evidence. But it did NOT graduate:
a just-crossed, regime-dependent edge goes on **PROBATION**, requiring confirmation across consecutive
new-data cycles. *Promote on evidence, confirm before you trust.*
